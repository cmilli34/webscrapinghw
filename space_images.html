    Colorful Mawrth Vallis
    This image acquired on February 7, 2019 by NASAs Mars Reconnaissance Orbiter, shows Mawrth Vallis, a place on Mars that has fascinated scientists because of the clays and other hydrated minerals detected from orbit.

  • March 4, 2019

    Colorful Impact Ejecta in Ladon Valles
    March 4, 2019
    Colorful Impact Ejecta in Ladon Valles
    This image acquired on January 23, 2019 by NASAs Mars Reconnaissance Orbiter, shows the western portion of a well-preserved (recent) impact crater in Ladon Basin.

  • March 4, 2019

    Gullies in Galle
    March 4, 2019
    Gullies in Galle
    This image acquired on December 26, 2018 by NASAs Mars Reconnaissance Orbiter, shows the hills that resulted from uplifted rocks due to an impact that formed the 230-kilometer diameter Galle Crater.

  • March 4, 2019

    The Slow Charm of Brain Terrain
    March 4, 2019
    The Slow Charm of Brain Terrain
    This image acquired on December 11, 2018 by NASAs Mars Reconnaissance Orbiter, shows a surface texture of interconnected ridges and troughs, referred to as brain terrain, found throughout the mid-latitude regions of Mars.

  • March 4, 2019

    Acidalia Colles - False Color
    March 4, 2019
    Acidalia Colles - False Color
    This image from NASAs Mars Odyssey shows part of Acidalia Colles. The term colles means hill.

  • March 1, 2019

    Near the River - False Color
    March 1, 2019
    Near the River - False Color
    This image from NASAs Mars Odyssey shows several channel features. Towards the top of the image are several stream-lined islands, created by liquid flow eroding preexisting rock.

  • February 28, 2019

    Protonilus Mensae - False Color
    February 28, 2019
    Protonilus Mensae - False Color
    This image from NASAs Mars Odyssey shows the hills that are part of Protonilus Mensae.

  • February 27, 2019

    Crater Dunes - False Color
    February 27, 2019
    Crater Dunes - False Color
    This image from NASAs Mars Odyssey shows the floor of an unnamed crater in Arabia Terra.

  • February 26, 2019

    Chryse Planitia - False Color
    February 26, 2019
    Chryse Planitia - False Color
    This image from NASAs Mars Odyssey shows the relative highlands of Arabia Terra near Chryse Plainitia.

  • February 25, 2019

    Chasma Boreale - False Color
    February 25, 2019
    Chasma Boreale - False Color
    This image from NASAs Mars Odyssey shows a large trough in the north polar cap near the head of Chasma Boreale.

  • February 22, 2019

    Curiosity on the Clay Unit
    February 22, 2019
    Curiosity on the Clay Unit
    NASAs Curiosity Mars rover took this image with its Mastcam on Feb. 10, 2019 (Sol 2316). The rover is currently exploring a region of Mount Sharp nicknamed Glen Torridon that has lots of clay minerals.

  • February 22, 2019

    Volcanic Plains - False Color
    February 22, 2019
    Volcanic Plains - False Color
    This image from NASAs Mars Odyssey shows a small part of the extensive volcanic plains located east of Olympus Mons.

  • February 21, 2019

    Marth Crater - False Color
    February 21, 2019
    Marth Crater - False Color
    This image from NASAs Mars Odyssey shows part of the floor of Marth Crater. Dark blue tones typically indicate basaltic sands.

  • February 20, 2019

    Following the Tracks
    February 20, 2019
    Following the Tracks
    This image acquired on January 13, 2019 by NASAs Mars Reconnaissance Orbiter, shows a cluster of dust devil tracks on the flat ground below the south polar layered deposits, but none on the layers themselves.

  • February 20, 2019

    Jumbled Blocks on the Floor of Melas Chasma
    February 20, 2019
    Jumbled Blocks on the Floor of Melas Chasma
    This image acquired on January 20, 2019 by NASAs Mars Reconnaissance Orbiter, shows a chaotic jumble of bright layered sediments, perhaps resulting from large landslides.

  • February 20, 2019

    Almost Like Water
    February 20, 2019
    Almost Like Water
    This image acquired on December 9, 2018 by NASAs Mars Reconnaissance Orbiter, shows Athabasca Valles with lava flows originating from Elysium Mons to the northwest.

  • February 20, 2019

    A Recent Impact Site in Noachis Terra
    February 20, 2019
    A Recent Impact Site in Noachis Terra
    This image acquired on December 9, 2018 by NASAs Mars Reconnaissance Orbiter, shows a recent impact in Noachis Terra in the southern mid-latitudes of Mars.

  • February 20, 2019

    Pasteur Crater - False Color
    February 20, 2019
    Pasteur Crater - False Color
    This image from NASAs Mars Odyssey shows part of the floor of Pasteur Crater. The deposit in the southwest part of the floor is a fill deposit that is undergoing erosion.

  • February 19, 2019

    Herschel Crater - False Color
    February 19, 2019
    Herschel Crater - False Color
    This image from NASAs Mars Odyssey shows part of the floor of Herschel Crater. Sand dunes are visible at the bottom of the image.

  • February 18, 2019

    Mawrth Vallis - False Color
    February 18, 2019
    Mawrth Vallis - False Color
    This image from NASAs Mars Odyssey shows Mawrth Vallis cutting across the center.

  • February 15, 2019

    Kaiser Crater Dunes
    February 15, 2019
    Kaiser Crater Dunes
    This image from NASAs Mars Odyssey shows several individual dunes located in Kaiser Crater. With continued winds sand dunes will move across the surface.

  • February 14, 2019

    High Latitude Dunes
    February 14, 2019
    High Latitude Dunes
    This image from NASAs Mars Odyssey shows a dune field on a crater floor in Terra Cimmeria. Dunes at high latitudes - near the polar caps - are affected by seasonal frost and ice.

  • February 13, 2019

    Looking Back on a Golden Opportunity
    February 13, 2019
    Looking Back on a Golden Opportunity
    In this navigation camera raw image, NASAs Opportunity Rover looks back over its own tracks on Aug. 4, 2010.

  • February 13, 2019

    Matara Crater Dunes
    February 13, 2019
    Matara Crater Dunes
    This image from NASAs Mars Odyssey shows the sand deposit on the floor of Matara Crater. The deposit is thick enough to hide the underlying crater floor creating a sheet of sand.

  • February 13, 2019

    HP3 on the Martian Surface
    February 13, 2019
    HP3 on the Martian Surface
    NASAs InSight lander set its heat probe, called the Heat and Physical Properties Package (HP3), on the Martian surface on Feb. 12, 2019.

  • February 12, 2019

    Crater Gullies
    February 12, 2019
    Crater Gullies
    This image from NASAs Mars Odyssey shows part of an unnamed crater in Noachis Terra. Several craters in the southern hemisphere contain floor fill that has subsequently been eroded to form depressions in the fill material.

  • February 11, 2019

    InSight Seismometer's Wind and Thermal Shield Seen from Space
    February 11, 2019
    InSight Seismometer's Wind and Thermal Shield Seen from Space
    NASAs InSight spacecraft and its recently deployed Wind and Thermal Shield were imaged on Feb. 4, 2019, by the HiRISE camera aboard NASAs Mars Reconnaissance Orbiter.

  • February 11, 2019

    Daedalia Planum
    February 11, 2019
    Daedalia Planum
    This image from NASAs Mars Odyssey shows part of the extensive volcanic flows that make up Daedalia Planum.

  • February 8, 2019

    Crater Dunes
    February 8, 2019
    Crater Dunes
    This image from NASAs Mars Odyssey shows sand dunes covering part of the floor of this unnamed crater in Noachis Terra.

    Claritas Fossae Channel
    February 7, 2019
    Claritas Fossae Channel
    This image from NASAs Mars Odyssey shows a section of an unnamed channel. This channel starts within Claritas Fossae and empties down hill into Icaria Planum.

  • February 6, 2019

    Crater Dunes
    February 6, 2019
    Crater Dunes
    This image from NASAs Mars Odyssey shows sand dunes covering part of the floor of this unnamed crater in Terra Cimmeria.

    Layered History

    Layered History
    This image acquired on December 8, 2018 by NASAs Mars Reconnaissance Orbiter, shows erosion of the surface revealing several shades of light toned layers, likely sedimentary deposits.
